Quiche Lorraine

About

Quiche Lorraine is a classic French tart that originated in the Lorraine region. This dish is celebrated for its creamy filling made with eggs, cream, bacon, and Gruyère cheese, all nestled in a flaky pastry crust.

Ingredients

  • 1 sheet of shortcrust pastry
  • 200g bacon, diced
  • 3 eggs
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1 cup grated Gruyère cheese
  • Salt, pepper, and nutmeg to taste

Way of Making

  1. Preheat oven to 180°C (350°F). Line a tart pan with the pastry and prick the base with a fork.
  2. Cook the bacon in a skillet until lightly crispy.
  3. Beat the eggs and mix in the cream, cheese, salt, pepper, and nutmeg.
  4. Spread the bacon on the pastry base, then pour the egg mixture over it.
  5. Bake for 30–40 minutes until golden and set. Cool slightly before serving.
