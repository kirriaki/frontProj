Coq au Vin

About

Coq au Vin is a classic French dish that exemplifies the art of slow cooking. It features chicken braised in red wine with aromatic vegetables, bacon, and herbs, creating a rich and hearty flavor.

Ingredients

  • 1 whole chicken, cut into pieces (or 4-6 chicken thighs/legs)
  • 2 cups red wine (preferably Burgundy)
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 150g bacon, diced
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 carrots, sliced
  • 200g mushrooms, sliced
  • 2 tbsp flour
  • 1 cup chicken stock
  • 2 tbsp tomato paste
  • 1 bouquet garni (thyme, bay leaf, parsley tied together)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Way of Making

  1. Marinate the chicken in wine, garlic, and herbs overnight.
  2. Remove the chicken from the marinade, pat dry, and season. Reserve the marinade.
  3. Heat olive oil in a pot, sauté the bacon, then set aside. Brown the chicken pieces in the same pot.
  4. Remove the chicken and sauté onion, carrots, and mushrooms. Sprinkle with flour and stir for 2 minutes.
  5. Add the marinade, chicken stock, and tomato paste. Return the chicken and bacon to the pot.
  6. Simmer on low heat for 1.5–2 hours until tender. Serve with crusty bread or mashed potatoes.
